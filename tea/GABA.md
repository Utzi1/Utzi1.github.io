# What is GABA?

![GABA](gaba.png)

Gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) is an inhibitory neurotransmitter produced by the decarboxylation of glutamate, catalyzed by glutamate decarboxylase (GAD). The isoforms GAD65 and GAD67 primarily regulate GABA levels in humans. Studies suggest that GABA intake can enhance concentration and promote relaxation.[^1][^2]

# GABA in Tea

Anaerobic fermentation of tea leaves increases GABA content. The main types of GABA tea are GABA oolong and GABA green tea. In Japan, green GABA tea with more than 410 mg of GABA per 100g of dry leaves is produced, notably by Toshihiro Hirayanagi at the Yamahira-en tea farm near Mount Fuji.

Such tea is valued for supporting concentration and relaxation.
This effect also is experienced by me, personally.

Japanese GABA teas do come with subtle notes of umami and sweetness, flavours vary depending on the tea cultivar and processing method. The GABA content in tea can vary widely, the alreay $410 mg/100g is quite high.
More often, consumers can expect around 100-200 mg of GABA per 100g of dry tea leaves, depending on the specific type and processing method. The actual GABA content in brewed tea will be lower due to dilution, but it can still provide a noticeable calming effect when consumed regularly.
But be carefull, some drinkers may experience tiredness.
