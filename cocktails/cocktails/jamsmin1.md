Due to regular gifts of *Zwetschgenwässerle*, a plum brandy made from home-grown plums,
I was able to experiment with this spirit.
It, ideally, not only exhibits the fruity and sweet character of the plums combined with
bitter-almond-notes.
These are due to the presence of amygdalin, a cyanogenic glycoside, which is found in the seeds of plums and other stone fruits.
This combines exceptionally good with the floral and slightly bitter character of jasmine tea.
I use a black jamsmine tea from Yunnan, scented with jasmine flowers that make up a 
well visible portion of petals in the black tea.

The Zwetschgenwasser is macerated with the tea for 2 to 3 hours, here 5 grams of tea per 100 ml of spirit are used.
After straining, the macerate is kept in the fridge a and ideally consumed sooner than later.

For a nice, stirred cocktail, I recommend:
  - 6 cl jasmine infused Zwetschgenwasser
  - 4 cl red Italian vermouth, I use *Bordiga di Torino*
  - 3 cl red orange curaçao
  - 1 barspoon of gentian bitters like *Rutaben Elixr* by *Cappelletti Novasalus*
  - 1 pinch of salt may be added

Its stirred on ice and strained into a chilled, Japanese, tall, tea cup.
Add an ice cube, if desired.
A garnish of a single jasmine flower is a nice touch, but not mandatory.

Be cautious with the Rutaben Elixr, that stuff is potent...
